Speakers

Dr. Manish Gupta

Director, Google Research India,
Infosys Foundation Chair Professor at IIIT Bangalore

Title of the talk:

Transforming Healthcare via Artificial Intelligence

Abstract:

We describe a clear need to transform healthcare systems across the world to achieve better health outcomes at affordable costs and additional challenges in countries like India of an acute shortage of doctors. We argue that this transformation is possible through a combination of a patient-centric approach and proactive care enabled by artificial intelligence (AI). We present examples of machine learning techniques to support more proactive care at various tiers of healthcare systems, including ICUs, secondary and primary healthcare centers, and more importantly, at home or work. In particular, we present our team's work on diabetic retinopathy screening using a convolutional neural network based approach and newly initiated work on preventing and managing cardio-vascular disease through lifestyle modifications and early medical interventions. We describe several outstanding challenges that need to be overcome in order to achieve meaningful impact in the real world.

Bio-sketch:

Dr. Manish Gupta is the Director of Google Research India, a new AI research lab recently announced by Google. He holds an additional appointment as Infosys Foundation Chair Professor at IIIT Bangalore. Previously, Manish has led VideoKen, a video technology startup, and the research centers for Xerox and IBM in India. As a Senior Manager at the IBM T.J. Watson Research Center in Yorktown Heights, New York, Manish led the team developing system software for the Blue Gene/L supercomputer. IBM was awarded a National Medal of Technology and Innovation for Blue Gene by US President Barack Obama in 2009. Manish holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Illinois at Urbana Champaign. He has co-authored about 75 papers, with more than 7,000 citations in Google Scholar (and an h-index of 45), and has been granted 19 US patents. While at IBM, Manish received two Outstanding Technical Achievement Awards, an Outstanding Innovation Award and the Lou Gerstner Team Award for Client Excellence. Manish is a Fellow of ACM and the Indian National Academy of Engineering, and a recipient of a Distinguished Alumnus Award from IIT Delhi.

Dr. Ramprasad Saptharishi

Faculty, School of Technology and Computer Science,
Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR)

Title of the talk:

Agent-based City Simulator for COVID-19 Modelling

Abstract:

The talk will provide an overview of epidemiological model for disease spread. Specifically, we will discuss why agent-based models are useful, the components of agent-based models, and the various issues related to their validation. We will then turn to COVID-19 and will survey a few agent-based models in use (that we are aware of). The talk will end with a discussion of the outcomes of the TIFR-IISc Agent-based City Simulator for Mumbai, and some studies on what may be happening in Mumbai.

Bio-sketch:

Ramprasad Saptharishi is a faculty in the School of Technology and Computer Science at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research. He did his undergraduation and obtained his Ph.D. from Chennai Mathematical Institute. He is a theoretical computer scientist who primarily works in the study of algebraic algorithms and coding theory. In the recent times, he has been a part of the TIFR-IISc COVID-19 simulation team, headed by Sandeep Juneja and Rajesh Sundaresan, towards attempting to model the spread of the disease.

Dr. Ratnik Gandhi

Senior Data Scientist, NTT Data, Vancouver, Canada

Title of the talk:

COVID-19 and Crude Oil Prices: AI based study on Economic Impact

Abstract:

The COVID-19 pandemic has caused worldwide disruptions to societies and to economies. Slowing economies are also impacting medical facilities and how madical infrastructure is used. Crude Oil prices, on the other hand, are a very good indicator of the economic conditions globally. Reducing economic impacts of the pandemic is a serious societal challenge. In this study, we investigate the problem of predicting oil prices under the light of the current crisis and study how coronavirus has changed oil markets.

Dr. Dhaval Patel

Assistant Professor, Ahmedabad University

Title of the talk:

Internet of Medical Things (IoMT): Architecture, Open Research Issues and Challenges

Abstract:

Ongoing COVID-19 pandemic has posed severe challenges and unveiled many opportunities across several domains, with Healthcare being prominently affected. The key challenges faced in Healthcare include patient diagnosis, tracking, quarantining and monitoring, hospital management, cross-contamination at the COVID hospitals, the safety of the frontline staff etc., Internet of medical things (IoMT), a particular case of the Internet of things (IoT) is a promising solution in Healthcare to effectively tackle the aforementioned challenges in real-time and at a massive scale. In this talk, the speaker will take you through the architecture of IoMT along with potential applications and use cases like IoT-enabled thermal screening, disease tracking through crowdsensing, UAV-based health surveillance and alerting, remote monitoring through assisted reproduction treatment (ART) to name few. Towards the end of the talk, the speaker will shed some light on the open research problems and challenges to implementing IoMT in daily practices.

Dr. Keyur D. Joshi

Assistant Professor, Ahmedabad University

Title of the talk:

COVID19 Recovery Model and Advanced Industries

Abstract:

Today, the effects of COVID-19 pandemic are well known across the community. Manufacturing industries are challenged because of the unexpected, strict and long-period of lockdown. During such times, the demand for essential items increases while non-essential items decreases. Industries require to work on a strategic model to recover from the pandemic situation as soon as possible. Such a recovery model with the help of an example, parameters, equations, inputs and outputs will be discussed. Industry 4.0 and Pharma 4.0 concept will be introduced with some case studies. The talk will end covering the effect of the pandemic on these concepts.

Bio-sketch:

Dr. Keyur D. Joshi is working as an assistant professor at School of engineering and applied sciences, Ahmedabad University, Gujarat, India. He has several research publications on topics such as mechatronics and machine vision. He is faculty advisor for student section of American society of mechanical engineers at the university and member of professional bodies such as INNS and ASME. His areas of interest include machine vision, computer vision, fuzzy logic, machine learning and artificial intelligence.
